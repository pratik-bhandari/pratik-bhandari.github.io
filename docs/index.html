Dr. Pratik Bhandari

Actively seeking job

I am in the job market in academia (postdoc position) as well as in industry (consultant, writer, data scientist, researcher). Get in touch by email (postdoc@pratikb.com or work@pratikb.com) if you have a position that you’d like to offer me or recommend. I’d highly appreciate any help.

I recently completed my PhD from Saarland University, where I studied psychology and language sciences. My research focused on understanding how we make sense of spoken language in adverse listening conditions. Before this, I worked on topics like metacognition and executive control in bilinguals. I used behavioural and electrophysiological methodologies, like eye-tracking and EEG, including online experiments/surveys. You can scroll down to see my academic qualification and selected publications, and click here to see the research projects I have undertaken so far across various domains of cognitive science, including psychology and psycholinguistics.

What I expect from a job and what I can contribute:

Academia: I wish to diversify my research fields and methodologies without making the postdoc research a continuity of the PhD. I intend to join a lab that does interdisciplinary research and fosters collaborations. There I would bring my training and experience in psycholinguistics and cognitive neuroscience and would expect opportunities to work with experts in other fields, like neuroimaging, computational modelling, and philosophy, to name a few. Questions about human and/or machine cognition, including language processing, memory, attention, metacognition, and (un)conscious awareness, would fit my current research interests.

Industry: I wish to use the skills and expertise I have gained to solve real-world problems; I expect the job to be challenging. I have over six years of experience and expertise in project management, academic writing, data analysis and visualisation, programming, and conducting research. I enjoy working with data (wrangling, statistical analysis, and visualisation), documenting codes, and writing reports and papers. My experience in academic research can be used for consultation — to make an informed and educated decision, especially if a company’s work revolves around applied health sciences, psychology, linguistics, or other similar areas.

There may be research domains or industry positions I have not mentioned above that can utilise my skills and expertise. Let’s talk about them and work together. I am just an email away!
